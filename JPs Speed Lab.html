Training Guidelines
⚡
Warm Up Every Time
Always begin with 5-10 min of arm circles, hip circles, leg swings, and dynamic stretches before sprint work.
💧
Stay Hydrated
Drink water before, during, and after every session. Young athletes dehydrate faster than adults.
🧠
Quality Over Quantity
Focus on perfect form over speed in the first 2 weeks. Better mechanics early = faster times later.
🎍
Make It Fun & Competitive
Use the stopwatch and challenge your athlete to beat their own time. Parent-child relays keep motivation high!
😴
Rest & Recovery
At least one full rest day between sessions. Sleep is the number one performance enhancer for growing athletes.
Sport-Specific Benefits

Football

  • Cone zigzag builds route-running quickness
  • Shuttle runs develop stop-start explosiveness
  • Lateral agility improves defensive footwork
  • Pogo jumps develop spring for jump balls

Golf

  • Single-leg balance improves stability through swing
  • Hip circles build hip rotation power
  • Core stability work improves clubhead speed
  • T-drill builds rotational athleticism

Basketball

  • Cone dribble drills improve ball-handling under pressure
  • Ladder quick feet improves crossover step speed
  • Tuck jumps build rise on shots and rebounds
  • Side shuffle improves on-ball defense positioning

Lacrosse

  • T-drill builds multi-directional cuts on field
  • Sprint and backpedal improves transition play
  • Single-leg bounding improves dodging defenders
  • Obstacle relay builds full game-speed movements
Summer Extension

After Week 6: Keep the Gains

Maintain 2-3 sessions per week using your favorite advanced drills. Increase reps, reduce rest times, or combine drills into circuits. Re-test benchmarks every 3-4 weeks. Goal: enter fall sports as the fastest, most agile athlete on the field!
